About

The Genotype-Phenotype Map is comprised of 3 distinct efforts

  • The data processing pipeline (which includes GWAS Upload)
  • An R package
  • The website and API

Nomenclature

We have defined these words as follows:

  • Study: Summary statistics related to a single trait or QTL measurement.
  • Trait: A collection of studies that are related to a single measurement. For example, a trait may have a rare variant study and a common variant study associated with it.
  • Trait Category: A manually curated map between a phenotypic trait and a higher level category. For example, "Height" would be mapped to "Anthropometric".
  • Study Extraction: A finemapped subset of a study that has a p-value below the cutoff of 1.5e-4 associated with it.
  • LD Block / Region: A region of the genome that has been segmented into smaller regions based on LD (using LDetect).
  • Colocalization: A statistical test that determines if a SNP is associated with a trait.
    • Coloc Pair: A pair of SNPs that are associated with a trait.
    • Coloc Group: A group of SNPs that are clustered based on coloc pairs.
    • Strong Coloc: A coloc group that includes coloc pairs with a posterior probability greater than 0.8.
    • Moderate Coloc: A coloc group that includes coloc pairs with a posterior probability greater than 0.6.
  • Rare Variant Analysis: A statistical test that determines if a SNP is associated with a trait.
  • Association: A statistical test that determines if a SNP is associated with a trait.
